Ms. Coco Lee

Born on January 17, 1975, in Hong Kong, China, Coco Lee, whose birth name was Li Meilin, was a luminary in the realm of Mandarin pop music and a versatile entertainer. She left an indelible mark on both the music and film industries, enchanting audiences with her ethereal voice and charismatic performances. Tragically, on July 5, 2023, Coco Lee passed away at the age of 48 due to unsuccessful treatment for depression, leaving behind a legacy that resonates deeply within the hearts of her fans.

A Star is Born

Coco Lee's musical odyssey began in 1993 when she participated in the 12th New Talent Singing Awards in Hong Kong, earning the title of first runner-up. This marked the commencement of a storied career that would encompass singing and acting, cementing her status as a true artist.

Musical Triumphs

In 1994, Coco Lee released her debut album "Seize the Moment of Love," catapulting her to the forefront of the music scene. Her self-titled album in 1996 soared to remarkable heights, securing second place on the Golden Melody Awards' annual sales chart. Her 1998 release "Indulgence" resonated across Asia, amassing an astounding 1.8 million copies sold. This pivotal year also witnessed her legendary "Adorable Coco" concert in Taiwan, where she became a darling of the masses.

A Global Presence

Venturing beyond linguistic boundaries, Coco Lee showcased her international appeal. In 1999, she lent her captivating vocals to the theme song "365 Days of Missing You" for the animated film "Lotus Lantern." The same year, she released her first English album "Just No Other Way," amassing a staggering 2 million global sales.

Beyond Music

Coco Lee's influence extended beyond melodies and harmonies. In 2001, she made history as the first Chinese artist to perform at the Oscars ceremony. Her foray into acting led to her starring role in the 2004 film "Give Me a Chance." Amid these accomplishments, Coco Lee's 2009 concert at the Walt Disney Concert Hall marked a momentous milestone as she became the first Chinese artist to hold a solo concert at this iconic venue.

A Lasting Legacy

Her 2016 victory as the "Queen of Singers" on the reality show "I Am a Singer Season 4" underscored her enduring musical prowess. Coco Lee's remarkable career was celebrated with the prestigious "Ultimate Song of the Year" award at the 2017 Asian Music Awards.

Remembering a Legend

Tragically, Coco Lee's radiant journey came to a premature end on July 5, 2023, when she lost her battle with depression. Her legacy, however, lives on through her unforgettable melodies, her impact on the entertainment industry, and the love of her devoted fans. Coco Lee, a luminary in every sense, will forever remain a shining star in the constellation of music and art.

“CoCo Lee is a true legend of the music industry. But just as importantly, she was kind, compassionate and a great friend and mentor to everyone. The only consolation … is her music will live on for artists and fans around the world in many generations to come.”

-- Jonathan Serbin, co-president of Warner Music Asia
